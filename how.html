How does it work?

wafer.space makes silicon manufacturing accessible by pooling multiple designs onto shared wafers, dramatically reducing costs for everyone.

The Manufacturing Process

Step 1: Submit Your Design

Upload your Project GDS files to wafer.space from anywhere in the world. Our platform accepts standard GDSII format files containing your integrated circuit layout.

Step 2: Design Consolidation

We tile multiple customer designs onto a single mask layout, creating a combined Mask GDS file. This shared approach is what makes the process cost-effective.

Step 3: Manufacturing at GlobalFoundries

The consolidated mask is sent to GlobalFoundries' Singapore facility where they create the photolithography masks and manufacture the silicon wafers using their proven GF180MCU process.

Step 4: Wafer Processing

Completed wafers return to wafer.space where they are diced (cut) into individual silicon die and sorted by design. Each customer receives their specific chips.

Step 5: Delivery

Your finished silicon die are packaged and shipped directly to you. You receive 500-1,000 raw, unpackaged die ready for your application or further packaging.

Why This Works

Traditional silicon manufacturing requires expensive mask sets that can cost tens of thousands of dollars. By sharing mask costs across multiple designs, wafer.space reduces individual project costs by up to 60%.

The process typically takes 4-6 weeks from mask submission to receiving your finished silicon, with yield rates of 85-95% depending on design complexity.
